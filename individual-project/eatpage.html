How to Eat Sustainably

Tip #1: Eat Less Meat

Studies show that animals are a huge producer of the world's carbon monoxide level. Cows produce almost double the amount of carbon monoxide as cars. It is a common misconception that protein only comes from animal products. Try limiting your meat intake by a few days a week, and substitute it for plant-based protein sources such as beans, quinoa, or tofu. Check out this article if you're not convinced.

Tip #2: Incorporate More Vegetables

By incorporating more vegetables into your diet, you are filling your caloric needs with more nutrient dense foods. Vegetables are full of fiber and other nutrients that your body needs to function. By eating more vegetables, you are lessening your need for other food groups. Maybe try replacing a starch as your side for dinner with a salad.

Tip #3: Prepare Smaller Portions

A big problem today is waste. People are constantly preparing or buying more food than they will actually eat. By having more food than you need, you are creating a higher demand. By preparing smaller portions of food, you are decreasing the chances of wasting food. Try making smaller portions of food for yourself, and grabbing a small snack afterwards if you are still hungry.

Tip #4: Eat Only Food with Credible Certified Standards

So many companies now rasie your groceries in unethical ways, a lot of those companies being meat companies. Not only can these conditions be bad for your personal health, they can also have negative impacts on the world. Try paying closer attention to the food your buying. Look out for words like, "organic" or "grass-fed".
